# Cover Letter Structure

Generate a U.S.-style cover letter with four paragraphs plus a professional header.

## Header

Use the same header style as the candidate's resume if the user provides one. If unavailable, use a clean placeholder format:

```text
[Candidate Name]
[Candidate Email] | [Candidate Phone] | [LinkedIn or Portfolio]

[Date]

[Recruiter Name or Hiring Team]
[Company Name]
[Company Office Address]
```

If recruiter name or address is missing, use:

```text
[Date]

Hiring Team
[Company Name]
```

## Paragraph 1: Introduction

Include:

- Who the candidate is
- The role they are applying for
- Why they are interested in this industry, company, or role

This paragraph may be moderately reusable, but it must still connect to the target role.

## Paragraph 2: Why This Company

Make this the most company-specific paragraph. Use specific research and at least two concrete details when sources are available.

Include one or more of:

- Culture or values with source support
- Specific product, project, initiative, business model, or strategic priority
- Recent company development
- Role-specific team priority
- Employee conversation or name-drop provided by the user

Avoid generic praise such as "prestigious company," "global impact," "innovative culture," or "fast-paced environment" unless grounded in specific evidence.

## Paragraph 3: Why This Candidate

Choose two core strengths and support each with one example from the user-provided candidate profile.

Do more than repeat the resume. Explain:

- Transferable learning
- Impact
- How the candidate approached problems
- How the candidate drove change or influenced outcomes
- Why the candidate's background is useful for this company and role

Do not fabricate experience, metrics, tools, responsibilities, or achievements.

## Paragraph 4: Thank You

Keep it concise, professional, and confident:

- Thank the reader
- Reaffirm interest in the role
- Express interest in discussing how the candidate can contribute

## Tone

- U.S. professional recruiting tone
- Clear, specific, and confident
- Not overly humble
- Not exaggerated
- Not poetic
- Not generic
- Concrete nouns and verbs
- Buzzwords only when grounded in evidence

## Length

Default to approximately 350-450 words unless the user specifies another limit.
