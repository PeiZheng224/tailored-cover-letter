# Role-Resume Match Framework

Compare the job description with the candidate information provided in the current invocation. Select only dimensions relevant to the role.

## Possible Dimensions

- Analytical skills
- Business judgment
- Product sense
- Strategy
- Data storytelling
- Cross-functional collaboration
- Leadership and influence
- Communication
- Client or stakeholder management
- Operational execution
- Entrepreneurial mindset
- Domain knowledge
- Adaptability across disciplines
- Technical or quantitative skills
- Research ability

## Match Strength

Use one of these labels:

- Strong: direct, recent, and well-supported evidence
- Medium: relevant evidence exists but may need translation or stronger detail
- Weak: adjacent or transferable evidence only
- Missing: no clear evidence provided

## Fit Matrix Format

```markdown
## B. Candidate-Company Match Matrix

| Role or company need | Candidate evidence | Strength | Cover letter framing |
|---|---|---:|---|
| [Need from JD or research] | [Evidence from provided profile] | [Strong/Medium/Weak/Missing] | [How to frame honestly] |
```

## Analysis Rules

- Use only candidate evidence provided in the current invocation.
- Do not force every dimension into the output.
- Do not invent missing metrics, titles, employers, tools, coursework, or projects.
- Do not simply restate resume bullets. Interpret why the experience matters for the target role.
- Highlight two strongest themes for the final letter's candidate paragraph.
- Mark any uncertain evidence as something the candidate should confirm.
