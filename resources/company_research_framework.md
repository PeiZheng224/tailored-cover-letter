# Company Research Framework

Research the target company from an analyst's perspective. Treat the job description as the source of truth for role-specific expectations.

## Source Priority

Prioritize:

1. Company official website
2. Investor relations pages
3. Annual reports, 10-K, S-1, shareholder letters, earnings calls, and press releases when available
4. Official company blog, engineering blog, product pages, newsroom, or careers page
5. Job description for role-specific expectations
6. Reputable business media as secondary context only

If web access is unavailable, ask the user to paste relevant company materials or proceed only from the job description and clearly state that external company research was not performed.

## Research Questions

Summarize only what is relevant to the cover letter:

- Business overview
- Business model and revenue logic
- Core products, services, or platforms
- Target customers or users
- Mission, values, and culture signals
- Strategic priorities
- Competitive moat or differentiation
- Recent initiatives, product directions, or market positioning
- Role-specific team priorities
- Specific company facts that connect to the candidate's background

## Evidence Rules

- Every company-specific claim must be sourced or clearly marked as an inference.
- Do not use generic claims such as "global impact," "prestigious company," "innovative culture," or "fast-paced environment" unless supported by specific evidence.
- Prefer concrete facts: product lines, customer segments, strategic initiatives, operating model, team mandate, stated values, or quoted priorities.
- Use secondary media only to supplement, not replace, official sources.
- Do not overstate certainty when source material is ambiguous.

## Research Brief Format

```markdown
## A. Research Brief

Sources consulted:
- [Source name]: [Link or source note]

Company overview:
- [Concise business summary]

Business model and customers:
- [Revenue logic, customer/user base, market]

Products, strategy, or initiatives:
- [Specific details relevant to the letter]

Culture or values signals:
- [Specific values or ways of working, with source note]

Role-specific priorities:
- [Needs from the JD]

Candidate connection opportunities:
- [Company fact] -> [Candidate background connection]
```
