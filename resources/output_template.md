# Output Template

Use this final response structure for every invocation.

```markdown
## A. Research Brief

Sources consulted:
- [Source name]: [Link or source note]

Company and role summary:
- [Concise analyst-style summary]

Specific details to use in the letter:
- [Detail 1]
- [Detail 2]

Source notes and inferences:
- [Claim]: [Source or "Inference from ..."]

## B. Candidate-Company Match Matrix

| Role or company need | Candidate evidence | Strength | Cover letter framing |
|---|---|---:|---|
| [Need] | [Evidence] | [Strong/Medium/Weak/Missing] | [Framing] |

## C. Bridge and Expansion Ideas

Include only if weak, missing, or non-obvious match areas exist.

| Weaker area | Honest bridge | Additional evidence to look for | Candidate can confirm or replace |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Gap] | [Bridge] | [Evidence] | [Verification] |

## D. Final Cover Letter

[Candidate Name]
[Candidate Email] | [Candidate Phone] | [LinkedIn or Portfolio]

[Date]

[Recruiter Name or Hiring Team]
[Company Name]
[Company Office Address]

Dear [Recruiter Name or Hiring Team],

[Paragraph 1: Introduction]

[Paragraph 2: Why this company]

[Paragraph 3: Why this candidate]

[Paragraph 4: Thank you]

Sincerely,

[Candidate Name]

## E. Customization Notes

- Verify [recruiter name/address/date].
- Confirm [company claim/source].
- Replace or confirm [candidate metric/project detail].
- Adjust [tone/length] if needed.
```

## Output Notes

- Keep the cover letter ready to edit, not over-explained.
- Include source links or clear source notes in the Research Brief.
- Do not include hidden reasoning or chain-of-thought.
- Keep customization notes short and practical.
